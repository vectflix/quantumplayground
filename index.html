üî≠ Mechanics & Fundamental Dynamics

1. Kinematics

  • Displacement: Vector change in position ($\Delta x$).
  • Velocity: Rate of change of displacement ($v = \Delta x / \Delta t$).
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity ($a = \Delta v / \Delta t$).

2. Newton's Laws of Motion

  • 1st Law: Inertia - Objects resist changes in motion.
  • 2nd Law: Fundamental law ($F = ma$).
  • 3rd Law: Action & Reaction are equal and opposite.

3. Work, Energy & Power

  • Work: Product of force and displacement ($W = Fd \cos \theta$).
  • Energy: Kinetic ($\frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and Potential ($mgh$).
  • Power: The rate of doing work ($P = W/t$).

9. Geometric Optics

7. Types of Chemical Bonds

  • Ionic: Complete transfer of electrons (Metal + Non-metal).
  • Covalent: Sharing of electron pairs (Non-metal + Non-metal).
  • Metallic: "Sea of electrons" surrounding positive ions.

8. Molecular Geometry (VSEPR)

  • Linear: 180¬∞ bond angle (e.g., $CO_2$).
  • Trigonal Planar: 120¬∞ bond angle (e.g., $BF_3$).

9. Polarity & Intermolecular Forces

  • Polar Bonds: Unequal sharing of electrons.
  • Dipole-Dipole: Attraction between polar molecules.
  • Hydrogen Bonding: Strong attraction involving H and N, O, or F.

‚öñÔ∏è Chemical Reactions & Stoichiometry

13. Types of Reactions

  • Synthesis: Two substances combine ($A + B \rightarrow AB$).
  • Decomposition: One substance breaks down ($AB \rightarrow A + B$).
  • Combustion: Reaction with $O_2$ producing $CO_2$ and $H_2$.

14. The Mole & Avogadro

  • The Mole: $6.022 \times 10^{23}$ particles (Avogadro's Number).
  • Molar Mass: Mass in grams of one mole of a substance.
  • Conversion: Moles to grams using $n = m / M$.

15. Yield & Limiting Reactants

  • Limiting Reactant: The substance used up first in a reaction.
  • Theoretical Yield: Maximum product possible from calculations.
  • Percent Yield: $(\text{Actual} / \text{Theoretical}) \times 100$.

19. Intro to Acids & Bases

  • Acids: Proton ($H^+$) donors; sour taste; $pH < 7$.
  • Bases: Proton ($H^+$) acceptors; bitter taste; $pH > 7$.
  • Neutralization: Acid + Base $\rightarrow$ Salt + Water.

20. Solubility Science

  • Solute: The substance being dissolved.
  • Solvent: The medium doing the dissolving (e.g., Water).
  • Saturation: When no more solute can dissolve at a given temp.

21. Titrations

  • Titration: Method to determine unknown concentration.
  • Equivalence Point: Moles of acid equal moles of base.
  • Indicators: Chemicals like Phenolphthalein that change color.

25. Thermochemistry

27. Chemical Equilibrium

  • Reversible Reactions: Occur in both directions.
  • Dynamic Equilibrium: Forward and reverse rates are equal.
  • Le Chatelier's Principle: Systems shift to oppose stress.

  • Prokaryotes: No nucleus; simple structure (e.g., Bacteria).

  • Equation: $6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{light} \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$.
  • Purpose: Converting light energy into chemical energy (Glucose).

7. DNA Structure

  • Nucleotides: Adenine-Thymine (A-T) and Cytosine-Guanine (C-G).

üåç Evolution & Ecosystems

13. Theory of Evolution

  • Variation: Individuals in a population have different traits.
  • Selection: Those with favorable traits survive and reproduce.
  • Adaptation: Over generations, traits that enhance survival become common.

14. Evidence of Common Ancestry

  • Homologous Structures: Similar anatomy in different species (e.g., whale fin and human arm).
  • Fossil Record: Chronological collection of life's remains in rock layers.
  • DNA Sequencing: Comparing genetic codes to determine relatedness.

15. Ecosystem Dynamics

  • Producers: Organisms that make their own food (Autotrophs).
  • Consumers: Organisms that eat others (Heterotrophs).
  • 10% Rule: Only about 10% of energy is passed to the next trophic level.

19. The Nervous System

  • Neurons: Cells that transmit electrical impulses.
  • Central Nervous System (CNS): The brain and spinal cord.
  • Synapse: The gap where neurotransmitters pass signals between neurons.

20. Gas Exchange & Transport

  • Hemoglobin: Protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen.
  • Alveoli: Tiny air sacs in lungs where gas exchange occurs.
  • Arteries vs. Veins: Arteries carry blood away from the heart; veins return it.

21. Immune Response

  • Innate Immunity: Immediate, non-specific defenses (skin, mucus).
  • Adaptive Immunity: Targeted response using B-cells and T-cells.
  • Antibodies: Proteins that identify and neutralize pathogens.

27. The Web of Life

  • Keystone Species: A species that has a disproportionately large effect on its environment.
  • Threats: Habitat loss, invasive species, and climate change.
