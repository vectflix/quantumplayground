Module 01

🔭 Mechanics & Fundamental Dynamics

1. Kinematics

  • Displacement: Vector change in position ($\Delta x$).
  • Velocity: Rate of change of displacement ($v = \Delta x / \Delta t$).
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity ($a = \Delta v / \Delta t$).

2. Newton's Laws of Motion

  • 1st Law: Inertia - Objects resist changes in motion.
  • 2nd Law: Fundamental law ($F = ma$).
  • 3rd Law: Action & Reaction are equal and opposite.

3. Work, Energy & Power

  • Work: Product of force and displacement ($W = Fd \cos \theta$).
  • Energy: Kinetic ($\frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and Potential ($mgh$).
  • Power: The rate of doing work ($P = W/t$).

4. Projectile Motion

Analyze the parabolic flight of objects under gravity.

5. Forces & Motion

Adjust friction and mass to see Newton's 2nd Law in action.

6. Gravity & Orbits

Explore the gravitational dance between celestial bodies.

🌊 Physics Part 2: Waves & Thermodynamics

7. Wave Properties

  • Transverse: Oscillation perpendicular to travel (Light).
  • Longitudinal: Oscillation parallel to travel (Sound).
  • Equation: $ v = f\lambda $ (Velocity = frequency × wavelength).
[Image of a transverse wave vs longitudinal wave]

8. Laws of Thermodynamics

  • 1st Law: Energy conservation ($\Delta U = Q - W$).
  • 2nd Law: Entropy of an isolated system always increases.
  • 0th Law: Thermal equilibrium concept.

9. Geometric Optics

  • Reflection: Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection.
  • Refraction: Bending of light (Snell's Law: $n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$).

10. Wave Interference

Observe constructive and destructive interference patterns.

11. Bending Light

Explore how light travels through different mediums and prisms.

12. Hooke's Law & SHM

Test the relationship between force, mass, and spring constants.

Module 03

⚡ Electricity & Magnetic Fields

13. Electrostatics

  • Charge: Measured in Coulombs ($C$).
  • Coulomb's Law: Force between charges ($F = k \frac{q_1q_2}{r^2}$).
  • Insulators vs Conductors: Ability for charge to flow.

14. Ohm's Law & Circuits

  • Ohm's Law: $V = IR$ (Voltage = Current × Resistance).
  • Series: Current is constant; voltages add up.
  • Parallel: Voltage is constant; currents add up.

15. Electromagnetism

  • Magnetic Fields: Created by moving charges (current).
  • Faraday's Law: Changing magnetic fields induce current.
  • Right-Hand Rule: Determining force and field direction.

16. Circuit Lab (DC)

Build virtual circuits with batteries, bulbs, and switches.

17. Charges & Fields

Visualize the invisible electric field lines around point charges.

18. Faraday's Law

Move a magnet through a coil to light up a bulb via induction.

Module 04

⚛️ Modern & Nuclear Physics

19. Atomic Structure

  • Rutherford: Discovered the dense, positive nucleus.
  • Bohr Model: Electrons exist in discrete energy levels.
  • Quantum Model: Electrons as probability clouds (orbitals).
[Image of the Bohr model of the atom vs the Quantum Mechanical model]

20. Nuclear Decay

  • Alpha ($\alpha$): Emission of a Helium nucleus.
  • Beta ($\beta$): Transformation of a neutron to a proton.
  • Half-Life: Time required for half of a sample to decay.

21. Special Relativity

  • Time Dilation: Time slows down at high speeds.
  • Mass-Energy: $E = mc^2$.
  • Photoelectric Effect: Light behaving as particles (photons).

22. Rutherford Scattering

Shoot alpha particles at atoms to see how the nucleus was discovered.

23. Build a Nucleus

Add protons and neutrons to create stable isotopes and observe decay.

24. Photoelectric Effect

Experiment with light intensity and frequency to eject electrons.

Module 05

🌌 Fluids & The Cosmos

25. Fluid Mechanics

  • Archimedes' Principle: Buoyant force equals weight of displaced fluid.
  • Bernoulli’s Principle: Fast moving air creates low pressure.
  • Pressure: $P = F/A$ (Force divided by Area).

26. The Gas Laws

  • Boyle's Law: Pressure is inversely proportional to Volume.
  • Ideal Gas Law: $PV = nRT$.
  • Absolute Zero: $0\text{ K}$ (where all molecular motion stops).

27. Cosmology & Stars

  • Stellar Life Cycle: From Nebula to White Dwarf or Supernova.
  • Black Holes: Regions of spacetime where gravity prevents light escape.
  • Expansion: Hubble’s Law and the Big Bang Theory.

28. Buoyancy

Drop objects into fluids to see why they float or sink based on density.

29. Gas Properties

Pump gas molecules into a box and observe effects of temperature and pressure.

30. My Solar System

Create your own planetary systems and watch orbital mechanics in real-time.

Module 01

🧪 Atoms, Molecules & States of Matter

1. Atomic Structure

  • Protons: Positive charge; determines identity.
  • Neutrons: Neutral charge; determines isotope.
  • Electrons: Negative charge; determines reactivity.

2. States of Matter

  • Solid: Fixed shape and volume; low energy.
  • Liquid: Fixed volume; takes shape of container.
  • Gas: No fixed shape or volume; high energy.
[Image of the molecular arrangement in solids, liquids, and gases]

3. The Periodic Table

  • Groups: Columns with similar chemical properties.
  • Periods: Rows representing electron shells.
  • Trends: Electronegativity and Ionization Energy.
